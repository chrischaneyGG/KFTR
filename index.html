Samoa’s Lone Rep in the 2026 IPP Class

Kaylan Faumui became the only athlete from Samoa selected for the NFL’s 2026 International Player Pathway class — one of just 15 picked globally.

Ten weeks of training. One pro day. A shot at an NFL roster. Faumui’s path is the version of the pipeline most fans never see: on-island talent identified by an international scout, lifted by family and placed into an accelerated development track.

One spot, on one list, can change a family’s trajectory for three generations.

The First NIL Check: Three Mistakes Athletes Make

The first real NIL opportunity can feel like a finish line. It is actually the start of a financial plan. Here are three conversations every athlete and family should have before the money arrives.

  • •Separate income from revenue. Taxes, fees and expenses can be sitting inside the number before the athlete ever spends a dollar.
  • •Put structure around the opportunity. Banking, documentation and professional guidance matter once money becomes consistent.
  • •Talk as a family early. In Pasifika families, money is family. A shared plan protects relationships and supports long-term goals.

The Seiler Group works with athletes and the families who raised them — helping turn opportunity into a plan for what comes next.
